Australian house price trends and forecasts for the last decade (Melbourne)

As the Australian real estate market continues to evolve, it becomes particularly important to understand its historical trends and current conditions. This visualization aims to shed light on house price changes in Australia, and Melbourne in particular, over the past decade, and the demographic and geographic factors associated with them. By analyzing population distribution, house price trends, house type share and geographic location together, investors, real estate developers and ordinary consumers can make decisions and strategies with greater clarity. Whether you are looking for an investment opportunity, considering a home purchase, or simply want to understand the dynamics of the market, this data can serve as a reference point.

Population distribution of Australian capital cities in 2021

Distribution of population size by Australian provincial capitals

According to the map it can be seen that the state with the highest population is New south Wales followed by Victoria . The majority of the population is located along the south east coast of Australia. The capital cities of these two states are Sydney and Melbourne respectively. Therefore, based on the map of population distribution it is concluded that the demand for housing is higher in the cities of Melbourne and Sydney. Therefore we focus on analyzing these two locations.

Percentage of House Types

The 2022 Melbourne Property Type Share pie chart shows that detached homes dominate the market at around 65%, indicating that Melbourne is still dominated by traditional forms of housing. Apartments account for around 22.5% of the market, reflecting the continued popularity of the urban lifestyle, particularly in the city center. In addition, townhouses and other dwelling types account for around 15%, providing some diversity and choice. This suggests that Melbourne's housing market is diversified and meets the housing needs of a range of people, from traditional family-oriented homes to urban lifestyle apartments. This trend may be influenced by urban growth, population movements and lifestyle choices.
